Product to Analyze: [Insert Product Here] Instructions: Analyze the product listed above as if it is the solution to a real-world problem. Assume: - Solution (what solves the problem) = The product - Process (how the solution works) = The product’s features, functions, or usage Your task is to reverse-engineer the context behind this product. Provide answers in the following sections: 1. Problem Identify the real-world problem that this product is designed to solve. - Focus on the underlying issue, not the product’s features. - Describe the problem in a human-centered way. 2. People Identify the group(s) who experience this problem. - Describe who is affected and why they are ideal users. - Mention behaviors, roles, situations, or characteristics. 3. Root Causes Identify the deeper reasons this problem exists for these people. - Explain underlying limitations, circumstances, or unmet needs. - Provide multiple causes if relevant. - Do not mention the product in the causes; stay focused on the people and their context. Output Requirements: - Be analytical, not descriptive. - Keep the three sections logically connected: Problem → People → Causes - Avoid listing features or describing the product unless necessary for context. - Treat the product strictly as the solution, not the topic of a product review.
STEP 2

20 Big A/cs and Search Keywords

I want to identify the most valuable prospective buyers for my solution so I can build a successful online promotion campaign. Please analyze everything thoroughly and provide clear, specific, and actionable insights. Using the information you gave me in your previous reply, please answer the following points in a detailed, structured, and example-rich way: 1. Prospective Buyer Segments (Target Audience Identification) * Which groups of people, professionals, or companies would benefit most from this solution? * What roles, industries, and seniority levels do they belong to? * What responsibilities do they have? * Which ones best match my preferred delivery method? * Explain why each segment is a strong match. 2. Their Problems & Pain Points (Needs and Demands) * What specific problems, challenges, or inefficiencies do these buyers experience? * How do these problems affect their goals, income, or operations? * Which of these problems can I help solve based on my solution? * Prioritize the problems by urgency or impact. 3. Topics & Issues They Discuss Online and on LinkedIn (Searchable Keywords) Provide very precise and practical outputs. * What topics, issues, questions, and frustrations do they talk about online? * What do they post about, comment on, complain about, or seek help with? * Provide: * keywords * long-tail phrases * hashtags * search terms * discussion themes * example post titles * Make the list ready for LinkedIn & Google search. 4. Their Desired Outcomes & Requirements (What They Want to Buy) * What results or improvements are they actively seeking? * What expectations do they have from an expert like me? * What features or delivery styles do they prefer? * What types of solutions are they willing to pay for? 5. Opportunity Analysis (What Offer I Should Create) * Based on their needs and my solution, what types of offers or services should I create? * Include options: done-for-you, consulting, coaching, training, digital products, productized services. * Rank opportunities from highest to lowest potential and explain why. 6. FAQs & Objections * What questions would they ask before deciding to work with someone like me? * What concerns, doubts, or objections might they have? * What proof or reassurance would they need? 7. Key Accounts to Follow (Market Monitoring & Trend Awareness) * Provide 20 major accounts, influencers, companies, or thought leaders in my niche. * Include: * their names * a short description of why they matter * their LinkedIn profile URLs (or website/social links if not on LinkedIn) * These should represent the best sources to observe market trends, industry conversations, and buyer behavior. Please ensure your full response is: * Highly structured * Practical and actionable * Supported with examples * Focused on real behavior, not generic marketing theory * Directly useful for understanding my audience, their needs, and my market
